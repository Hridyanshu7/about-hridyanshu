The Problem

Every AI system at Voxar depends on accurate transcripts of vernacular call recordings — but production had no validation layer:

5 vernacular languages
Hindi, Kannada, Marathi, Tamil & Telugu transcripts feed every downstream AI system at Voxar.
No ground truth
Gemini transcripts had misattributed speakers, drifted timestamps & hallucinated text — nothing to measure against.
2–3 hrs per call
Audio, text editing & storage lived in separate apps with no synced playback — labelling a 15-min call took hours.
My Role as AI-native IC PM
Tool Design & Build
Designed and built a mini-SaaS labelling platform via Claude Code in 4–5 hours — fetching audio and draft transcripts from AWS S3 in real time, with a hotkey-driven editor for message text, timestamps, and speaker attribution.
LLM-as-Judge Validation
Built a cross-model comparison feature — Gemini 2.5 Flash vs Claude Sonnet 4.6 and other models — surfacing alternate interpretations for ambiguous segments to validate ground truth.
Hands-On Labelling
Personally labelled ~10–12 hours of Hindi transcripts, iterating on the tool in production before rolling it out to a wider team.
Freelancer Enablement
Rolled the tool out to native-speaker freelance labellers across 5 languages — completing 125 hours of validated ground-truth data with resumable, trackable progress.
