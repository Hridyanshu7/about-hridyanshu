The Problem

CAM (Credit Appraisal Memo) reports are the backbone of MSME lending decisions — yet production was broken. Analysts worked from handwritten notes, memory, and hour-long recordings they had to manually replay. Each report took 2–3 hours to write, yet output quality was inconsistent: judgment varied analyst to analyst, the role carried high attrition making training futile, and vetters had to repeatedly re-validate work. Critical signals — business ground realities, income patterns, behavioural cues — never appeared in bank statements or credit reports, and were routinely missed or inconsistently captured.

My Role as Founding PM
UX Architecture Designed the LOS interview window from scratch — an organic, SOP-guided UI that minimised analyst cognitive load while ensuring full topic coverage across every interview.
Prompt Engineering Owned all 100+ prompts end-to-end — defining inferential and reasoning logic for each CAM section: borrower family, business, assets/liabilities, income streams, and future plans.
Eval Framework Built a ground-truth dataset with client champions; ran iterative qualitative evals on real transcripts, consulting clients async on a secure channel for ambiguous edge cases.
Client Collaboration Extracted credit domain knowledge from 5 NBFC/SFB clients and translated their SOPs into customized prompt logic — no two clients share the same configuration.

Beyond the Numbers

The deeper win was standardization of intelligence. The AI layer consistently surfaced signals invisible to document-based underwriting — business ground realities, income patterns, and behavioural cues from the interview. Clients reported reasoning that was more consistent and auditable than any analyst had ever produced. With high analyst attrition making training a poor investment, this was a structural fix, not a productivity hack. 2 new NBFC clients (Finodaya Capital, Inprime Finance) came onboard in the months following launch.
